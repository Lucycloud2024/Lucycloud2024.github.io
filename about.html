About Me

The story behind the engineer — where I come from, what drives me, and where I am going.

My Story

My name is Ebelechukwu Lucy Okafor — a Cloud and DevOps Engineer based in Nigeria with a deep passion for building systems that are not just functional, but observable, scalable, and secure.

My journey into DevOps began with a simple curiosity: how does software actually get from a developer's laptop to a live production system? That question led me down a path that now spans cloud infrastructure, container orchestration, CI/CD automation, and distributed systems observability.

In January 2026 I joined the DevOps Micro Internship (DMI) Cohort 2 — a 14-week intensive programme run by The Cloud Advisory. Over six months I went from networking fundamentals all the way to deploying a production-grade 8-service microservices application on AWS EKS with full CI/CD and observability. I graduated in May 2026.

As the App / Docker Lead on the DMI Group 5 team project, I was responsible for containerising all 8 microservices, managing Docker image builds and tagging, validating the local stack, and pushing images to AWS ECR for the Kubernetes team to deploy. Working in a team of 11 engineers across 6 disciplines taught me as much about collaboration as it did about technology.

I hold the Google Cloud Professional Cloud Security Engineer certification — a testament to my commitment to not just deploying systems, but securing them.

I believe that DevOps is not a job title. It is a philosophy — one that bridges the gap between writing code and running it reliably at scale. My goal is to build infrastructure that developers can trust and users never notice, because it simply works.

My Values

  • 01. Document everything. If it is not written down, it did not happen. Every deployment, every error, every fix deserves a record.
  • 02. Automate the repeatable. Manual processes are error-prone. If you do something twice, it should be scripted by the third time.
  • 03. Security is not an afterthought. Credentials in code, unsecured ports, and unmonitored access are not technical debt — they are risks waiting to become incidents.
  • 04. Understand before you operate. Running a command without knowing what it does is how outages happen. I always ask why before I run.
  • 05. Observability is not optional. You cannot fix what you cannot see. Metrics, traces, and logs are not extras — they are the eyes of your system.
  • 06. Teams win together. The best infrastructure is built by people who communicate clearly, review each other's work, and share knowledge freely.
